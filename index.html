What is Compactkit? Compactkit is a set of free, privacy-first file compressors that run entirely in your web browser. Whether you need to compress a PDF, compress audio, or compress video, your file is processed on your own device using WebAssembly and is never uploaded to a server. There is no sign-up, no watermark, and no file-size paywall.

Why on-device compression matters

Most free online compressors upload your file to their servers to process it. For a contract, a bank statement, a personal recording, or a private video, that means handing a sensitive file to a third party you don't control. Compactkit is different: it uses WebAssembly to run real compression engines — Ghostscript for PDFs and FFmpeg for audio and video — directly inside your browser. Nothing is transmitted, so it's safe even for confidential documents, and it keeps working offline once the page has loaded.

Which tool do I need?

Compress a PDF

Use the PDF compressor to shrink scanned documents and image-heavy PDFs so they fit under email size limits. Image-heavy PDFs often drop 50–90% on the smallest setting.

Compress audio

Use the audio compressor to reduce voice memos, podcasts, and music (MP3, M4A, WAV, AAC) by lowering the bitrate — useful for emailing recordings or freeing up storage.

Compress video

Use the video compressor to make MP4 and MOV clips smaller for messaging and upload. In-browser video compression is best for short clips; for longer videos the native app is faster.
