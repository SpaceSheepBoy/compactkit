Help & FAQ

How to make audio smaller safely

Pick the right setting for voice memos, interviews, podcasts, or music.

Short answer: To compress an audio file for free without uploading it, choose your file above, pick voice or music quality, and click Compress. The smaller audio file downloads instantly and your original never leaves your device. On iPhone, do the same in Safari or install the free Audio Compressor app for a simple phone workflow.
How do I compress audio, step by step?
  1. Choose your audio file. Click Choose audio above or drag a file in. MP3, M4A, WAV, AAC, and voice memos all work.
  2. Pick a bitrate. Lower bitrate = smaller file. 48–64 kbps is ideal for voice; 128 kbps is a good balance for music; 192 kbps keeps more detail.
  3. Click Compress. The audio is re-encoded in your browser and the smaller M4A downloads automatically.

Why compress in the browser instead of uploading?

Most free online audio compressors upload your file to their servers. For personal recordings, interviews, or unreleased music, that means handing your audio to a third party. This page compresses the audio in your browser, so your recording is not sent to another company just to make it smaller.

How do I compress audio on iPhone?
  • In Safari: open this page on your iPhone, choose a file from Files or your voice memos, and compress it in the browser.
  • With the app: install the free Audio Compressor app to make recordings smaller and share them faster from your iPhone.
Is it really free?

Yes — the online tool is free with no sign-up, no watermark, and no file-size paywall.

Are my files uploaded?

No. Compression happens entirely in your browser. Your audio never leaves your device.

What file do I get back?

A compressed M4A (AAC) file, which plays on iPhone, Android, Mac, and Windows and is smaller than the equivalent MP3.
