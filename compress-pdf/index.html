Help & FAQ

How to make a PDF smaller safely

Pick the right quality level and avoid uploading private documents.

Quick tip: Choose PDF. Pick quality. Compress.
How do I compress a PDF, step by step?
  1. Choose your PDF. Click Choose PDF above or drag a file in. It stays on your device.
  2. Pick a quality level. Screen gives the smallest file, ebook is the best all-round choice for email and sharing, printer keeps the most detail.
  3. Click Compress. Just Compress makes a smaller PDF and downloads it automatically.
What do the quality levels mean?
SettingImage resolutionBest for
Screen72 dpiEmail, web upload, smallest size
Ebook150 dpiGeneral sharing — recommended default
Printer300 dpiPrinting, keeping image detail
Why compress in the browser instead of uploading?

Many online PDF compressors upload your file first. Just Compress keeps your PDF on your device, which is better for contracts, bank statements, IDs, and medical records.

How do I compress a PDF on iPhone?

iPhones have no built-in PDF compressor. Two free options:

  • In Safari: open this page on your iPhone, choose your PDF from Files, and compress it directly in the browser.
  • With the app: install the free Just PDF Compressor app to compress many PDFs at once, work offline, and also sign and merge documents.
Is it really free?

Yes — the online tool is free with no sign-up and no file-size limit paywall. Output PDFs include a small Just Compress app logo watermark.

Are my files uploaded?

No. Your PDF stays on your device.

How much smaller will my PDF get?

Image-heavy PDFs often shrink 50–90% on the screen setting. Text-only PDFs are already small and won't change much.

Does it work on Mac, Windows, and Android?

Yes. It works on modern phones and computers. The app is for iPhone and iPad.
